Welcome to Kintal

Kintal (from kin = person + tal = speak) is a constructed language designed for maximum clarity and ease of learning.

Design Principles

  • Maximum clarity: Words in same contexts are maximally distinct
  • Minimal error-prone: Avoid similar-sounding roots
  • Efficiency: Short roots (1-2 syllables) for common concepts
  • Phonetic consistency: Each letter always pronounced the same
  • Compositional vocabulary: Build complex words from simple roots (e.g., kintal = person-speak = language, firkol = fire-color = red)

Story: The Lost Child

smalkin pas go fra pat. ka pas vol vis birim in bigvitlok. sol go, en lun go sur vinsur. smalkin vis. ka es patnul. ka ne vis kor kan dom es. ka tal, "mi es patnul! kim mi pas go fra pat? mi vol go min dom!" den, ka her dog. kan dog! dog go pa ka. dog vis pat. smalkin go met kan dog, fra bigvitlok pa dom. ka fut es ne patnul rep.
A child went from the path. He/she wanted to see birds of the forest. The sun went, and the moon went over the sky. The child saw. He/she is lost. He/she doesn't see where his/her house is. He/she said, "I am lost! Why did I go from the path? I want to go to my home!" Then, he/she heard a dog. His/her dog! The dog went to him/her. The dog saw the path. The child went with his/her dog, from the forest to the house. He/she will not be lost again.

Rulebook

Phonology

Type Letters Details
Consonants p, t, k, m, n, s, f, l, r, h, v 11 consonants total
Vowels a, e, i, o, u 5 vowels total
Syllable Structure (C)V(C) Optional consonant, required vowel, optional consonant
Pronunciation Guide:
• a = "ah" (as in father)
• e = "eh" (as in bed)
• i = "ee" (as in machine)
• o = "oh" (as in note)
• u = "oo" (as in boot)
Stress and Tone: Not phonemic. Stress and tone do not change word meaning. Speakers may freely use them for emphasis, emotion, or rhythm.

Grammar

Word Order: SVO (Subject-Verb-Object)
Basic pattern: Subject - (Tense) - Verb - Object
Example: mi hav pan = I have food
With tense: tu pas go dom = you went home
Plural: Add suffix -im to nouns
• kin → kinim (person → people)
• dom → domim (house → houses)
• pan → panim (food → foods)
Tense: Use particles before the verb
• pas = past tense
• fut = future tense
• (unmarked) = present/timeless

Examples:
• mi pas go dom = I went home
• tu fut hav pan = you will have food
• na go dom = we go home (present)
Negation: Use ne before the verb
Pattern: Subject - (Tense) - ne - Verb - Object
• mi ne hav pan = I don't have food
• tu pas ne go dom = you didn't go home
Possession (Pronouns): Add suffix -n to pronouns
• mi → min (my)
• tu → tun (your)
• ka → kan (his/her/their)
• na → nan (our)
• nu → nun (your - plural)

Usage: min dom = my house

Possession (Nouns): Use in marker
Pattern: Noun₁ - in - Noun₂ = Noun₂ of Noun₁
Example: kin in dom = person's house (house of person)
Adjectives: Follow the noun
Pattern: Noun - Adjective
• dom big = big house
• pan hot = hot food
• mi hav pan hot = I have hot food
Comparatives: Use mas (more) or les (less) with dan (than)
Pattern: Noun₁ - mas/les - Adjective - dan - Noun₂
• dom mas big dan dom = this house is bigger than that house
• mi mas big dan tu = I'm bigger than you
• tu les big dan ka = you're smaller than him/her
Superlatives: Use max (most) or lest (least)
Pattern: Noun - max/lest - Adjective
• dom max big = biggest house
• kin max rap = fastest person
• dom lest big = smallest house
Questions: Replace unknown element with question word
• ko = who
• ket = what
• kor = where
• ken = when
• kim = why
• kom = how

Examples:
• ko go dom? = who goes home?
• tu vis ket? = what do you see?
• kin es kor? = where is the person?
Tag Questions: Add ne? at the end of any statement
Pattern: [Complete Statement] + , ne?

Examples:
• tu go dom, ne? = you're going home, aren't you?
• es bon, ne? = it's good, isn't it?
• ka pas vis so, ne? = he saw this, didn't he?
• na fut tal, ne? = we'll speak, won't we?

This pattern works universally, regardless of tense or verb.
Compound Words: Combine roots to create new words
Pattern: Modifier + Head (no spaces, no hyphens)
• kintal = person-speak = language
• akdom = water-house = bathroom
• panlok = food-place = restaurant
• firkol = fire-color = red
• smalkin = small-person = child
• bigvitlok = tree-place = forest
Prepositions:
• pa = to, for (recipient, benefactive)
• fra = from (source, origin)
• met = with (accompaniment, instrument)
• sur = over, above (spatial position)
• ins = in, inside, within

Examples:
• mi mak dom pa tu = I make house for you
• mi go fra dom = I go from house
• mi go met tu = I go with you
• bir go sur dom = bird goes over house

Numbers

Core Digits (0-10): nul, un, du, tri, for, fif, sik, sep, ok, nin, des
Large Numbers: sen (100), mil (1000)

Building Compound Numbers:
• 11-19: des + digit (des-un = 11, des-du = 12)
• 20, 30, 40...: digit + des (du-des = 20, tri-des = 30)
• Complex: du-des-for = 24 (2-tens-4)
• Hundreds: tri-sen-du-des-fif = 325 (3-100-2-tens-5)

Examples:
• 15 = des-fif
• 23 = du-des-tri
• 156 = un-sen-fif-des-sik
• 2000 = du-mil
